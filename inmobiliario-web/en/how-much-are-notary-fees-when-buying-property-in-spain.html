How Much Are Notary Fees When Buying Property in Spain

Notary fees in Spain typically range from 0.5% to 1% of the property value, varying by location and property price. Understanding these mandatory costs helps foreign buyers budget accurately for their Spanish property purchase.

Publicidad

Buying property in Spain is an exciting venture, but many international buyers find themselves confused about the additional costs involved beyond the purchase price. One of the most commonly misunderstood expenses is notary fees, which are mandatory in Spanish property transactions. Understanding how much you'll need to pay for notary services and what these fees cover is essential for accurate budgeting and avoiding unpleasant surprises during the closing process.

What Are Notary Fees in Spanish Property Transactions?

Notary fees in Spain are regulated by the Spanish government and are calculated based on a sliding scale according to the property's declared value. These fees typically range between €600 and €1,500 for most residential properties, though luxury properties with higher values will incur greater costs. The notary (notario) is a public official who verifies the legality of the transaction, ensures all parties understand the contract terms, and registers the deed officially. Unlike in some countries, Spanish notaries are impartial and don't represent either the buyer or seller.

Breakdown of Costs When Buying Property in Spain

When calculating how much are notary fees when buying property in Spain, it's important to understand they're part of a larger package of expenses. The total additional costs typically amount to 10-15% of the property's purchase price. For a resale property, you'll pay transfer tax (ITP) of 6-10% depending on the region, plus notary fees, land registry fees (€400-€800), legal fees (typically 1% of purchase price plus VAT), and gestor fees if applicable. For new properties, you'll pay VAT (IVA) at 10% instead of transfer tax, plus stamp duty (AJD) at 1-1.5%.

The notary fee structure follows a decreasing percentage scale: higher for the first portion of the property value and decreasing for subsequent amounts. This means that while a €200,000 property might incur €800 in notary fees, a €500,000 property won't cost proportionally more—perhaps around €1,200.

Common Mistakes Buyers Make Regarding Notary Fees

  • Assuming notary fees are negotiable—they're set by law and cannot be discounted
  • Confusing notary fees with lawyer fees, which are separate professional services
  • Failing to budget for the complete package of closing costs beyond just the notary
  • Expecting the notary to provide legal advice—they ensure legality but don't advocate for either party
  • Not requesting an itemized estimate of all costs before the signing appointment
  • Believing that notary fees are included in the property price
  • Overlooking additional notary costs for power of attorney documents if not attending personally

Additional Considerations for International Buyers

International buyers should obtain an NIE (foreigner identification number) before the purchase, which may involve additional notary costs of around €50-€100 if executed through a notary. If you cannot attend the signing in person, you'll need to grant power of attorney, which requires notarization both in your home country and in Spain, potentially adding €200-€500 to your expenses.

Are notary fees in Spain paid by the buyer or seller?

In Spain, notary fees are traditionally paid by the buyer, as they're considered part of the acquisition costs. However, some aspects may be negotiated between parties, though this is uncommon practice.

Can I choose my own notary when buying property in Spain?

Yes, the buyer has the legal right to select the notary for the transaction. The notary must be licensed to practice in the province where the property is located, but you can choose among qualified professionals in that area.

Do notary fees vary between different regions of Spain?

No, notary fees are regulated at the national level and follow the same official scale throughout Spain. However, other costs like transfer taxes do vary significantly by autonomous community.

Understanding how much are notary fees when buying property in Spain helps you budget accurately for your purchase. While these fees are mandatory and non-negotiable, they're a relatively small part of the total closing costs and ensure your transaction is legally sound and properly registered. Always request a detailed breakdown of all costs from your lawyer or real estate agent before proceeding with any property purchase in Spain.
